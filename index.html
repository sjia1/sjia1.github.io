Curriculum Vitae

I am a Research Scientist in SCOT Labs, under SCOT-forecasting at Amazon. Both my recent academic and industry research focus on A/B testing ("labs" as we call it at Amazon), especially in complex systems involving challenges such as interference or non-stationarity. More broadly, I am interested in probability, statistics, optimization, and machine learning problems that arise from and provide insights into real-world challenges.

My research has been recognized with the INFORMS Pierskalla Best Paper Award in Health Applications and the George Dantzig Dissertation Award in Operations Research and Management Science.

Previously, I was an Assistant Research Professor in the Center for Data Science for Enterprise and Society (CDSES) at Cornell University. I received my PhD in Algorithms, Combinatorics and Optimization (ACO) from CMU. My doctoral research focused on online decision-making (a condensed version of my PhD thesis: Learning and Earning Under Noise and Uncertainty). I also received my MS in Applied Math and Statistics from Stony Brook University and BS in Mathematics from Tsinghua University.

Outside research I like to play sports, especially soccer, basketball, table tennis etc.
